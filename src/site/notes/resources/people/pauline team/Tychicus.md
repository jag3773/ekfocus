## Summary

Tychicus came from the Roman province of Asia ([[resources/bible/gospels/Acts 20#20:4\|Acts 20#20:4]]). He first appears in the delegation carrying the collection for the saints from Greece through Macedonia to Jerusalem ([[resources/bible/gospels/Acts 20#20:4\|Acts 20#20:4]]), which places him among respected figures in the Asian churches before Paul's imprisonment.

Paul called him "the beloved brother and faithful servant and fellow bondservant in the Lord" ([[resources/bible/epistles/Colossians 4#4:7\|Colossians 4#4:7]]) and "the faithful minister in the Lord" ([[resources/bible/epistles/Ephesians 6#6:21\|Ephesians 6#6:21]]). The Greek term *diakonos* puts Tychicus in the same functional category as [[resources/people/pauline team/Paul\|Paul]], [[resources/people/pauline team/Timothy\|Timothy]], [[resources/people/pauline team/Apollos\|Apollos]], [[resources/people/pauline team/Epaphras\|Epaphras]], and [[resources/people/pauline team/Phoebe\|Phoebe]], all identified as *diakonoi* serving the early church in leadership roles.

Tychicus carried at least two of Paul's prison epistles. He delivered the letter to the [[Colossian Church\|Colossian Church]] with [[resources/people/pauline team/Onesimus\|Onesimus]] ([[resources/bible/epistles/Colossians 4#4:7-9\|Colossians 4#4:7-9]]) and almost certainly brought the letter to the [[Ephesian Church\|Ephesian Church]] too ([[resources/bible/epistles/Ephesians 6#6:21-22\|Ephesians 6#6:21-22]]). As Paul's coworker, he wasn't only tasked with carrying the letter but with strengthening and encouraging the congregations he visited. He was a critical bridge of communication and exhortation in the expanding early church.

Paul sent or planned to send Tychicus to Ephesus ([[resources/bible/epistles/2 Timothy 4#4:12\|2 Timothy 4#4:12]]), probably to relieve [[resources/people/pauline team/Timothy\|Timothy]] so Timothy could rejoin Paul in Rome. Paul also considered sending either [[resources/people/pauline team/Artemas\|Artemas]] or Tychicus to Crete to replace [[resources/people/pauline team/Titus\|Titus]] ([[resources/bible/epistles/Titus 3#3:12\|Titus 3#3:12]]), which shows Paul trusted Tychicus as an equipping servant just as he did Titus.

Tychicus may not have had a strong apostolic gifting as we don't see him starting churches in new areas but he did function as an equipping servant alongside Paul.
