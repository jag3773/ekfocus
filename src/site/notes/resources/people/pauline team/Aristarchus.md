## Summary

Aristarchus (name means "best ruler") was a Macedonian from [[Thessalonian Church\|Thessalonica]] who traveled with [[resources/people/pauline team/Paul\|Paul]] through some of the most dangerous episodes in Paul's ministry. He probably came to faith during Paul's initial work in Thessalonica.

He first appears during the Ephesus riot. The silversmith Demetrius provoked a mob over the perceived threat to the cult of Artemis, and the crowd seized Aristarchus and [[resources/people/pauline team/Gaius\|Gaius]], "Paul's traveling companions from Macedonia," and dragged them into the theater ([[resources/bible/gospels/Acts 19#19:29\|Acts 19#19:29]]). Aristarchus endured mob violence on Paul's behalf before his recorded ministry had even begun.

He and [[resources/people/pauline team/Secundus\|Secundus]] represented the [[Thessalonian Church\|Thessalonian Church]] in the delegation carrying the collection for the saints from Greece to Jerusalem ([[resources/bible/gospels/Acts 20#20:4\|Acts 20#20:4]]).

Aristarchus boarded the ship with Paul when he was transported as a prisoner to Rome ([[resources/bible/gospels/Acts 27#27:2\|Acts 27#27:2]]). Whether he traveled as a voluntary companion or in some official capacity is debated, but Paul calls him "my fellow prisoner" (*synaichmalōtos*) in [[resources/bible/epistles/Colossians 4#4:10\|Colossians 4#4:10]]. The term could be literal (Aristarchus was also in custody) or he may have voluntarily shared the constraints of Paul's confinement. Paul uses this same rare term for only [[resources/people/pauline team/Epaphras\|Epaphras]] ([[resources/bible/epistles/Philemon 1#1:23\|Philemon 1#1:23]]) and [[resources/people/pauline team/Andronicus\|Andronicus]] and [[resources/people/pauline team/Junia\|Junia]] ([[resources/bible/epistles/Romans 16#16:7\|Romans 16#16:7]]).

In [[resources/bible/epistles/Philemon 1#1:24\|Philemon 1#1:24]], Paul lists Aristarchus among his "fellow workers" with [[resources/people/pauline team/John Mark\|John Mark]], [[resources/people/pauline team/Demas\|Demas]], and [[resources/people/pauline team/Luke\|Luke]], confirming his presence during Paul's Roman imprisonment.

From the Ephesian riot to the Roman prison, Aristarchus stayed with Paul through circumstances that drove others away. [[resources/people/pauline team/Demas\|Demas]] deserted ([[resources/bible/epistles/2 Timothy 4#4:10\|2 Timothy 4#4:10]]). Aristarchus did not.
