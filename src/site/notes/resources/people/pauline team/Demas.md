## Summary

Demas appears first as a trusted coworker and last as a deserter. Three verses across three letters tell the story.

In [[resources/bible/epistles/Philemon 1#1:24\|Philemon 1#1:24]], written during Paul's first Roman imprisonment (c. 60-62 AD), Demas is listed among Paul's "fellow workers" with [[resources/people/pauline team/John Mark\|John Mark]], [[resources/people/pauline team/Aristarchus\|Aristarchus]], and [[resources/people/pauline team/Luke\|Luke]]. The term *synergos* (fellow worker) is Paul's standard designation for active ministry partners; it implies shared labor. In [[resources/bible/epistles/Colossians 4#4:14\|Colossians 4#4:14]], written around the same time, Paul includes Demas in his greetings alongside "Luke, the beloved physician." The pairing with [[resources/people/pauline team/Luke\|Luke]] and the placement in Paul's inner circle show Demas held a respected position in the Roman ministry team.

In [[resources/bible/epistles/2 Timothy 4#4:10\|2 Timothy 4#4:10]], Paul's last letter before his execution (c. 66-67 AD), Paul writes: "Demas, in love with this present world (*ton nyn aiōna*), has deserted (*egkateleipen*) me and gone to Thessalonica." The verb *egkataleipō* means to abandon or forsake, the same word used in the Psalms for being forsaken by God ([[resources/bible/writings/Psalm 22#22:1\|Psalm 22#22:1]]). Demas fell in love with the present age, the world-system opposed to the age to come that Paul's ministry proclaimed (see the warning in [[resources/bible/epistles/James 4#4 4\|James 4#4 4]]).

Thessalonica was a prosperous Greco-Roman city. Demas may have come from there, returning home when the cost of staying with Paul grew too steep. Paul's second imprisonment was harsher than the first: a dungeon instead of a rented house, execution instead of acquittal.

The context sharpens the point. Paul mentions Demas right after saying "only [[resources/people/pauline team/Luke\|Luke]] is with me" ([[resources/bible/epistles/2 Timothy 4#4:11\|2 Timothy 4#4:11]]). [[resources/people/pauline team/Crescens\|Crescens]] went to Galatia, [[resources/people/pauline team/Titus\|Titus]] to Dalmatia, [[resources/people/pauline team/Tychicus\|Tychicus]] to Ephesus. Those departures were assignments. Demas left because he loved the world.
