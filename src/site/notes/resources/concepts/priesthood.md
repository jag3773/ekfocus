The role assigned to Israel ([[resources/bible/pentateuch/Exodus 19#19:6\|Exodus 19#19:6]]) and transferred to the church ([[resources/bible/epistles/1 Peter 2#2:9\|1 Peter 2#2:9]], [[resources/bible/epistles/Revelation 1#1:6\|Revelation 1#1:6]]): mediating God's presence to the world. Priests stood between God and the people. They offered sacrifices, maintained the [[resources/concepts/temple\|temple]], and taught God's law.

The teaching function gets repeated emphasis in Scripture. [[resources/bible/pentateuch/Leviticus 10#10:11\|Leviticus 10#10:11]] assigns it. [[resources/bible/pentateuch/Deuteronomy 33#33:10\|Deuteronomy 33#33:10]] confirms it. [[resources/bible/writings/2 Chronicles 17#17:9\|2 Chronicles 17#17:9]] records Levites carrying it out across Judah. [[resources/bible/prophets/Malachi 2#2:7\|Malachi 2#2:7]] calls the priest "the messenger of the Lord of hosts" whose lips should guard knowledge. When priests failed at this, Malachi condemned them for showing partiality ([[resources/bible/prophets/Malachi 2#2:9\|Malachi 2#2:9]]) and Ezekiel condemned them for feeding themselves rather than the flock ([[resources/bible/prophets/Ezekiel 34#34:2-4\|Ezekiel 34#34:2-4]]).

In the OT, priestly garments could transmit [[resources/concepts/holiness\|holiness]] to unprepared people ([[resources/bible/prophets/Ezekiel 44#44:19\|Ezekiel 44#44:19]]). The priests had to change clothes before entering common spaces. Jesus reversed this dynamic: his holiness flowed outward to cleanse rather than contaminate ([[resources/bible/gospels/Luke 8#8:44\|Luke 8#8:44]]).

Peter transfers the priestly identity to the church: believers are "a royal [[resources/concepts/priesthood\|priesthood]], a holy nation" ([[resources/bible/epistles/1 Peter 2#2:9\|1 Peter 2#2:9]]), drawing on [[resources/bible/prophets/Isaiah 43#43:20-21\|Isaiah 43#43:20-21]] and [[resources/bible/pentateuch/Exodus 19#19:6\|Exodus 19#19:6]]. The purpose remains the same: declare the excellencies of the God who called you.
