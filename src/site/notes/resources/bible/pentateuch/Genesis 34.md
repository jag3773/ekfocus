# Genesis 34

###### 34:1

Now Dinah, the daughter Leah had borne to Jacob, went out to visit the daughters of the land.

###### 34:2

When [[resources/concepts/Shechem\|Shechem]] son of Hamor the Hivite, the prince of the region, saw her, he took her and lay with her by force.

###### 34:3

And his soul was drawn to Dinah, the daughter of Jacob. He loved the young girl and spoke to her tenderly.

###### 34:4

So [[resources/concepts/Shechem\|Shechem]] told his father Hamor, “Get me this girl as a wife.”

###### 34:5

Jacob heard that [[resources/concepts/Shechem\|Shechem]] had defiled his daughter Dinah, but since his sons were with his livestock in the field, he remained silent about it until they returned.

###### 34:6

Meanwhile, [[resources/concepts/Shechem\|Shechem]]’s father Hamor came to speak with Jacob.

###### 34:7

When Jacob’s sons heard what had happened, they returned from the field. They were filled with grief and fury, because [[resources/concepts/Shechem\|Shechem]] had committed an outrage in Israel by lying with Jacob’s daughter—a thing that should not be done.

###### 34:8

But Hamor said to them, “My son [[resources/concepts/Shechem\|Shechem]] longs for your daughter. Please give her to him as his wife.

###### 34:9

Intermarry with us; give us your daughters, and take our daughters for yourselves.

###### 34:10

You may settle among us, and the land will be open to you. Live here, move about freely, and acquire your own property.”

###### 34:11

Then [[resources/concepts/Shechem\|Shechem]] said to Dinah’s father and brothers, “Grant me this favor, and I will give you whatever you ask.

###### 34:12

Demand a high dowry and an expensive gift, and I will give you whatever you ask. Only give me the girl as my wife!”

###### 34:13

But because [[resources/concepts/Shechem\|Shechem]] had defiled their sister Dinah, Jacob’s sons answered him and his father Hamor deceitfully.

###### 34:14

“We cannot do such a thing,” they said. “To give our sister to an uncircumcised man would be a disgrace to us.

###### 34:15

We will consent to this on one condition, that you become circumcised like us—every one of your males.

###### 34:16

Then we will give you our daughters and take your daughters for ourselves. We will dwell among you and become one people.

###### 34:17

But if you will not agree to be circumcised, then we will take our sister and go.”

###### 34:18

Their offer seemed good to Hamor and his son [[resources/concepts/Shechem\|Shechem]].

###### 34:19

The young man, who was the most respected of all his father’s household, did not hesitate to fulfill this request, because he was delighted with Jacob’s daughter.

###### 34:20

So Hamor and his son [[resources/concepts/Shechem\|Shechem]] went to the gate of their city and addressed the men of their city:

###### 34:21

“These men are at peace with us. Let them live and trade in our land; indeed, it is large enough for them. Let us take their daughters in marriage and give our daughters to them.

###### 34:22

But only on this condition will the men agree to dwell with us and be one people: if all our men are circumcised as they are.

###### 34:23

Will not their livestock, their possessions, and all their animals become ours? Only let us consent to them, and they will dwell among us.”

###### 34:24

All the men who went out of the city gate listened to Hamor and his son [[resources/concepts/Shechem\|Shechem]], and every male of the city was circumcised.

###### 34:25

Three days later, while they were still in pain, two of Jacob’s sons (Dinah’s brothers Simeon and Levi) took their swords, went into the unsuspecting city, and slaughtered every male.

###### 34:26

They killed Hamor and his son [[resources/concepts/Shechem\|Shechem]] with their swords, took Dinah out of [[resources/concepts/Shechem\|Shechem]]’s house, and went away.

###### 34:27

Jacob’s other sons came upon the slaughter and looted the city, because their sister had been defiled.

###### 34:28

They took their flocks and herds and donkeys, and everything else in the city or in the field.

###### 34:29

They carried off all their possessions and women and children, and they plundered everything in their houses.

###### 34:30

Then Jacob said to Simeon and Levi, “You have brought trouble upon me by making me a stench to the Canaanites and Perizzites, the people of this land. We are few in number; if they unite against me and attack me, I and my household will be destroyed.”

###### 34:31

But they replied, “Should he have treated our sister like a prostitute?”
