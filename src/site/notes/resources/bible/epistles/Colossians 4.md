# Colossians 4

###### 4:1

Masters, supply your slaves with what is right and fair, since you know that you also have a Master in heaven.

###### 4:2

Devote yourselves to prayer, being watchful and thankful,

###### 4:3

as you pray also for us, that God may open to us a door for the word, so that we may proclaim the mystery of Christ, for which I am in chains.

###### 4:4

Pray that I may declare it clearly, as I should.

###### 4:5

Act wisely toward outsiders, redeeming the time.

###### 4:6

Let your speech always be gracious, seasoned with salt, so that you may know how to answer everyone.

###### 4:7

[[resources/people/pauline team/Tychicus\|Tychicus]] will tell you all the news about me. He is a beloved brother, a faithful minister, and a fellow servant in the Lord.

###### 4:8

I have sent him to you for this very purpose, that you may know about us, and that he may encourage your hearts.

###### 4:9

With him I am sending [[resources/people/pauline team/Onesimus\|Onesimus]], our faithful and beloved brother, who is one of you. They will tell you about everything here.

###### 4:10

My fellow prisoner [[resources/people/pauline team/Aristarchus\|Aristarchus]] sends you greetings, as does [[resources/people/pauline team/John Mark\|Mark]] the cousin of [[resources/people/pauline team/Barnabas\|Barnabas]]. You have already received instructions about him: If he comes to you, welcome him.

###### 4:11

[[resources/people/pauline team/Jesus Justus\|Jesus, who is called Justus]], also sends greetings. These are the only Jews among my fellow workers for the kingdom of God, and they have been a comfort to me.

###### 4:12

[[resources/people/pauline team/Epaphras\|Epaphras]], who is one of you and a servant of Christ Jesus, sends you greetings. He is always wrestling in prayer for you, so that you may stand mature and fully assured in the full will of God.

###### 4:13

For I testify about him that he goes to great pains for you and for those at Laodicea and Hierapolis.

###### 4:14

[[resources/people/pauline team/Luke\|Luke]], the beloved physician, and [[resources/people/pauline team/Demas\|Demas]] send you greetings.

###### 4:15

Greet the brothers in [[Laodicea\|Laodicea]], as well as [[resources/people/pauline team/Nympha\|Nympha]] and the church that meets at her house.

###### 4:16

After this letter has been read among you, make sure that it is also read in the church of the Laodiceans, and that you in turn read the letter from [[Laodicea\|Laodicea]].

###### 4:17

Tell [[resources/people/pauline team/Archippus\|Archippus]]: “See to it that you complete the ministry you have received in the Lord.”

###### 4:18

This greeting is in my own hand—Paul. Remember my chains. Grace be with you.
